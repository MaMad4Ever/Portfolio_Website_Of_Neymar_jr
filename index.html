About Neymar

Hometown

Neymar was born in Mogi das Cruzes, São Paulo, Brazil. His father was also a football player.

Brilliant Talent

He showed great talent from a young age, joining Santos FC at 11, where he began developing his football skills.

Global Popularity

Neymar is one of the most popular athletes worldwide with millions of social media followers.

Football Career

Brazil National Team (2010-Present)

Star player and captain of the Brazilian national team:

  • 126 appearances, 79 goals (2nd all-time scorer)
  • Copa América champion (2019)
  • Olympic Gold Medal (2016)
  • FIFA Confederations Cup (2013)
  • 2014 World Cup (4th place)

Santos (2009-2013)

Neymar started his career at Brazilian club Santos and quickly became a star. During this period:

  • Copa do Brasil champion (2010)
  • Copa Libertadores champion (2011)
  • South American Footballer of the Year (2011)
  • Campeonato Paulista (2010, 2011, 2012)

Barcelona (2013-2017)

Joined Barcelona in 2013 and formed the famous MSN trio (Messi, Suárez, Neymar):

  • La Liga champion (2015, 2016)
  • UEFA Champions League champion (2015)
  • Copa del Rey champion (2015, 2016, 2017)
  • FIFA Club World Cup (2015)
  • Supercopa de España (2013)

Paris Saint-Germain (2017-2023)

Joined Paris Saint-Germain in a record-breaking transfer:

  • Ligue 1 champion (2018, 2019, 2020, 2022)
  • Coupe de France champion (2018, 2020, 2021)
  • Coupe de la Ligue (2018, 2020)
  • UEFA Champions League runner-up (2020)
  • Trophée des Champions (2018, 2019, 2020)

Al Hilal (2023-2024)

Joined Al Hilal in Saudi Arabia in August 2023:

  • Saudi Super Cup champion (2023)
  • Record transfer to Saudi Pro League
  • Brazil's all-time top scorer in national team history
  • Global ambassador for Saudi football

Santos (2025)

Returned to his boyhood club Santos FC in early 2025 after time in Saudi Arabia

  • Possible mentoring role for young talents
  • Club legend homecoming
  • Finishing career where it all began

UEFA Champions League

FIFA Confederations Cup
2013

Copa Libertadores

Led Santos to their first Copa Libertadores title since Pelé's era, scoring in the final.

FIFA Puskás Award
2011

Neymar's Puskás Award (2011) was given by FIFA for the most beautiful goal of the year—an incredible solo goal against Flamengo, featuring multiple dribbles from midfield to finish.
